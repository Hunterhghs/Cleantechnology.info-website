Introduction

What Is Clean Technology?

Clean technology refers to any process, product, or service that reduces negative environmental impacts through significant energy efficiency improvements, sustainable use of resources, or environmental protection activities.

⚡

Energy Generation

Solar, wind, hydroelectric, geothermal, and nuclear fusion technologies that produce electricity with minimal or zero carbon emissions and environmental degradation.

Energy Storage

Advanced battery systems, hydrogen fuel cells, pumped hydro, and emerging storage technologies that enable reliable energy supply from intermittent renewable sources.

Electric vehicles, hydrogen-powered transit, sustainable aviation fuel, and smart mobility solutions that are decarbonizing how we move people and goods globally.

Why It Matters

The Case for Clean Technology

Clean tech isn't just an environmental imperative — it's the greatest economic opportunity of the 21st century.

🌡️
Climate Crisis

Addressing Global Warming

The planet has warmed by approximately 1.2°C since the pre-industrial era. Clean technologies are essential to limiting warming to 1.5°C and avoiding the most catastrophic impacts of climate change.

  • Energy production accounts for 73% of global greenhouse gas emissions
  • Renewables can reduce power sector emissions by 90% by 2050
  • Every $1 invested in clean energy yields $3–$8 in economic benefits
💰
Economic Growth

Trillions in Market Opportunity

The global clean technology sector is projected to exceed $2.5 trillion annually by 2030. Countries and companies leading the transition are gaining decisive competitive advantages.

  • Solar energy costs have dropped 90% since 2010
  • Clean energy employs over 40 million people worldwide
  • Green bonds market surpassed $500 billion in annual issuance
